# Rubric: Research Projects

Total: 100 points

## 1. Research question and significance - 15 points
- `13-15`: The project asks a precise, consequential question with a credible case for why the
  answer would matter.
- `9-12`: The question is meaningful but the significance or novelty case needs sharpening.
- `5-8`: The project has an identifiable theme but lacks a sharply defined question.
- `0-4`: The project does not present a coherent research agenda.

## 2. Technical rigor - 25 points
- `22-25`: Mathematical claims, empirical methods, and implementation details are explicit,
  reproducible, and appropriately cautious.
- `16-21`: The technical foundation is mostly sound but some assumptions or controls are underdone.
- `9-15`: The project has a serious technical attempt but important validity gaps remain.
- `0-8`: Technical claims are unsupported or methodologically weak.

## 3. Use of evidence - 20 points
- `18-20`: Evidence matches the claim type. Empirical work uses credible baselines or ablations,
  and essay-style work uses accurate sources, examples, and counterarguments.
- `13-17`: Evidence is relevant but uneven, with one major missing comparison, source, or stress
  test.
- `7-12`: Evidence is partial or weakly aligned with the main claims.
- `0-6`: Evidence is too thin to sustain the argument.

## 4. Speculative quality and scope control - 20 points
- `18-20`: Speculative or interpretive claims are clearly labeled, precisely mapped to canonical
  ML concepts, and bounded by stated limitations.
- `13-17`: The project is mostly disciplined but occasionally overreaches or leaves mappings vague.
- `7-12`: The project mixes established and speculative claims without enough control.
- `0-6`: Speculation substitutes for technical reasoning.

## 5. Originality and future-work value - 10 points
- `9-10`: The project contributes a genuinely fresh angle, method sketch, or synthesis that could
  support follow-on work.
- `6-8`: The project is thoughtful but closer to a strong synthesis than an original contribution.
- `3-5`: The project mostly reorganizes known material without adding a clear new angle.
- `0-2`: Original contribution is not apparent.

## 6. Communication and publication readiness - 10 points
- `9-10`: The paper is well organized, properly cited, transparent about authorship and IP, and
  close to review-ready.
- `6-8`: The argument is readable but needs revision in structure, citation quality, or appendix
  detail.
- `3-5`: The project communicates the main idea but lacks publication-style polish.
- `0-2`: The final artifact is incomplete or not reviewable.

## Minimum completeness conditions
The submission cannot earn above `69/100` unless it:

- distinguishes canonical claims from interpretive or speculative claims;
- includes a reproducible appendix or an explicit justification for why reproducibility is not
  applicable;
- documents sources, collaborators, and authorship boundaries; and
- states concrete limitations and next research steps.

## Instructor note
Reward ambition only when it is paired with precision. A bold research claim with weak controls
should score below a narrower but well-supported project.
