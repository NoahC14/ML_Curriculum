# Project: Cross-Domain Transfer Analysis Through Structural Comparison

## Problem statement
Study transfer between two domains, tasks, or architectures and ask whether structural comparison
helps predict when transfer will succeed. The project may focus on representation reuse, pretraining
transfer, fine-tuning behavior, or failure under distribution shift.

Candidate settings include:

- vision model transfer across medical and natural-image domains;
- language-model transfer across code and natural language tasks;
- graph model transfer across molecular and social-network settings;
- reinforcement-learning policy transfer across related environments.

## Research emphasis
This prompt targets students who want to connect empirical transfer learning with category-theoretic
ideas about structure preservation and Unity Theory themes of coherence across transformation without
letting those themes replace standard evidence.

## Suggested guiding questions
- What notion of transfer is being evaluated: zero-shot reuse, fine-tuning, feature reuse, policy
  adaptation, or another clearly defined mechanism?
- What structural similarities and differences exist between source and target settings?
- Which invariants, symmetries, or compositional regularities appear to matter?
- Can the analysis predict failure as well as success?

## Suggested readings
- The core module most relevant to the chosen domain pair.
- Module 15 on evaluation under shift and robustness.
- Module 16 on structure, invariance, and transfer.
- One to three transfer-learning or domain-adaptation papers in the selected area.

## Recommended deliverables
- a `6-10` page report;
- a transfer setup with source and target tasks described precisely;
- comparison figures or tables showing where transfer helps or breaks; and
- a structural discussion that distinguishes exact correspondences from loose analogy.

## Evaluation criteria
- source and target domains are described with enough technical detail for comparison;
- transfer claims are supported by explicit evidence rather than intuition alone;
- structural analysis improves explanation or hypothesis generation;
- negative transfer and mismatch cases are discussed; and
- the final paper is suitable for a research seminar or internal review.

## Scope notes
- Students should avoid claiming universal transfer principles from a single narrow experiment.
- If the project includes Unity Theory language, keep it in a marked companion section.
