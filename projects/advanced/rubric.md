# Rubric: Advanced Projects

Total: 100 points

## 1. Problem framing and scope discipline - 15 points
- `13-15`: The question is precise, nontrivial, and matched to the available data, compute, and
  timeline. Assumptions, risks, and success criteria are explicit.
- `9-12`: The question is worthwhile but scope, assumptions, or success criteria need tightening.
- `5-8`: The project goal is understandable but too broad, too narrow, or weakly operationalized.
- `0-4`: The project lacks a coherent research question or feasible scope.

## 2. Technical rigor - 30 points
- `26-30`: Mathematical setup, modeling choices, and implementation details are explicit and
  defensible. Baselines, controls, and failure modes are handled seriously.
- `18-25`: The technical core is mostly sound but some assumptions, derivations, or controls are
  thin.
- `10-17`: The project contains substantial effort but major gaps remain in method validity or
  implementation discipline.
- `0-9`: Technical claims are not supported by a sound method.

## 3. Evidence and evaluation quality - 25 points
- `22-25`: Evaluation matches the question, uses credible metrics or analyses, and distinguishes
  claims from speculation. Limitations are discussed directly.
- `16-21`: Evidence is useful but one important baseline, metric, or robustness check is missing.
- `9-15`: Results are partial or difficult to interpret because the evaluation design is weak.
- `0-8`: Evidence is too incomplete to support the stated conclusions.

## 4. Structural synthesis - 15 points
- `13-15`: Category-theoretic or structural language clarifies composition, invariance, transfer,
  or architecture design without replacing standard ML reasoning.
- `9-12`: Structural ideas are relevant but not fully integrated into the empirical or mathematical
  story.
- `5-8`: Structural framing is present mostly as metaphor or loose commentary.
- `0-4`: Structural framing is absent or misleading.

## 5. Communication and research craftsmanship - 15 points
- `13-15`: The report is publication-style, reproducible, well organized, and clear about
  limitations, related work, and authorship.
- `9-12`: The report is readable and mostly complete but some sections or citations are thin.
- `5-8`: The report communicates the main idea but lacks research polish or reproducibility detail.
- `0-4`: The final artifact is incomplete or not meaningfully reviewable.

## Minimum completeness conditions
The submission cannot earn above `69/100` unless it:

- states a concrete research question;
- includes reproducible technical artifacts;
- evaluates at least one nontrivial baseline or comparison; and
- discusses limitations and next steps explicitly.
