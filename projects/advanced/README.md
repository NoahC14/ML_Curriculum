# Advanced Projects

Advanced projects are the highest-structure open-ended track in the repository. They should
produce publication-style final submissions with enough scaffolding to guide topic selection,
method design, and evaluation without collapsing into a recipe.

## Track purpose
Use this track for students who are ready to synthesize multiple core ML modules and the category
theory companion layer in a technically serious way. Typical outputs combine formal framing,
experimental evidence, and a well-scoped research narrative.

## Submission expectations
Each submission should include:

- a project proposal with a concrete question, baselines, and risk register;
- two advisor-style check-ins, one before implementation lock and one before final writing;
- a final report written in publication style with abstract, related work, method, results,
  limitations, and references;
- reproducible artifacts such as notebooks, code, figures, or appendices; and
- an authorship note identifying collaborators, borrowed assets, and any external assistance.

## IP and authorship policy
- Students retain authorship of original analysis, code, and writing unless a separate course or
  institutional policy says otherwise.
- External papers, datasets, figures, code, and model weights must be cited precisely.
- If a project appears suitable for public release or workshop submission, the final report should
  state who owns the reusable artifacts and who must approve publication.

## Prompt set
- [`categorical-architecture-analysis/`](./categorical-architecture-analysis/README.md):
  analyze a modern architecture through categorical structure and empirical comparison.
- [`structural-interpretability-study/`](./structural-interpretability-study/README.md):
  study interpretability through invariance, composition, and representation structure.

## Evaluation
Use [`rubric.md`](./rubric.md) for track-level grading. Advanced projects are graded primarily on
technical rigor, evidence quality, and disciplined scope control.
